The Space Coast industrial market has quietly become one of the most compelling stories in Florida commercial real estate. While investor attention has historically focused on South Florida, Tampa, and Orlando, Brevard County's industrial fundamentals have reached a tipping point that demands attention.

## Sub-4% Vacancy Tells the Story

Industrial vacancy in Brevard County has compressed to below 4% — a figure that signals genuine supply constraints rather than cyclical tightness. When vacancy drops this low, landlords gain meaningful pricing power, and tenants face limited options for relocation or expansion.

For investors, this means two things: strong occupancy protection for existing assets, and a favorable environment for lease rollovers at fair market rents.

## Aerospace and Defense Drive Demand

The demand side of the equation is equally compelling. The Space Coast is home to an extraordinary concentration of aerospace and defense tenants — companies that require specialized industrial and flex space for manufacturing, assembly, and logistics.

Major employers including SpaceX, Blue Origin, L3Harris, Lockheed Martin, and Northrop Grumman continue to expand their footprints in the region. Each expansion creates ripple effects across the supply chain, driving demand for small-bay warehouse and flex industrial space that serves subcontractors and support operations.

## NNN Structures Protect the Downside

One of the most attractive features of Space Coast industrial assets is the prevalence of triple-net (NNN) lease structures. These leases shift operating expenses — property taxes, insurance, and maintenance — to the tenant, providing investors with clean, predictable cash flow.

Combined with strong going-in cap rates (often 8% or higher for well-positioned assets), NNN industrial properties on the Space Coast offer a rare combination of yield and downside protection.

## Looking Ahead

With population growth outpacing the national average, unemployment below the national rate, and billions in committed aerospace investment, the structural tailwinds for Space Coast industrial real estate show no signs of abating.

Rising Tide Property Group is actively pursuing industrial acquisitions in the market — targeting small-bay warehouse and flex assets with strong tenancy, NNN lease structures, and clear paths to rent growth through near-term lease rollovers.
